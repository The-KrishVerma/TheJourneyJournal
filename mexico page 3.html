Mexico City Itinerary (7-10 Days)

DAY 1: Centro Histórico and Zócalo

Centro Histórico: Begin your journey at the heart of Mexico City, the historic center. Wander through narrow cobblestone streets lined with colonial-era buildings, vibrant plazas, and traditional shops. Marvel at the stunning baroque architecture of the Metropolitan Cathedral, the largest cathedral in Latin America. Inside, you'll find intricate woodwork, stunning altarpieces, and a deep sense of history. Don’t forget to explore the bustling side streets, where you’ll discover artisan shops and quaint cafes.

Zócalo (Plaza de la Constitución): This iconic square serves as the city's cultural and political hub. With the stunning backdrop of the National Palace adorned with Diego Rivera murals and the Templo Mayor’s Aztec ruins, this is an essential stop. On weekends, cultural performances and lively street markets bring the plaza to life. It's an ideal place to experience the rich tapestry of modern and ancient Mexican traditions.

DAY 2: Chapultepec Park and Museum of Anthropology

Chapultepec Park: One of the largest urban parks in the world, Chapultepec Park offers a serene retreat from the city's bustling streets. Take a leisurely walk through its lush green trails, relax by its tranquil lakes, or explore its many monuments and sculptures. You can even rent a paddleboat to enjoy the water or visit vendors selling traditional snacks like elotes (corn on the cob).

National Museum of Anthropology: Located within Chapultepec Park, this world-renowned museum is a treasure trove of pre-Columbian artifacts. Home to iconic exhibits like the Aztec Sun Stone and the Mayan artifacts from Chichén Itzá, it provides a fascinating insight into Mexico's ancient civilizations. Spend hours exploring its beautifully curated galleries and expansive outdoor spaces.

DAY 3: Teotihuacán Pyramids

Teotihuacán Pyramids: Take a day trip to Teotihuacán, the "City of the Gods," located just outside of Mexico City. Marvel at the breathtaking Pyramid of the Sun and Pyramid of the Moon, which offer panoramic views of the surrounding landscape. Walk down the Avenue of the Dead, exploring ancient ruins and vibrant murals that depict the rich culture and history of this ancient civilization. Guided tours are highly recommended to gain a deeper understanding of this UNESCO World Heritage site.

DAY 4: Coyoacán and Frida Kahlo Museum

Coyoacán: Stroll through the colorful streets of this bohemian neighborhood, known for its vibrant markets and artisan stalls. Relax at the leafy Plaza Hidalgo while savoring traditional churros and coffee.

Frida Kahlo Museum: Visit "La Casa Azul," the former home of renowned artist Frida Kahlo. Explore her personal belongings, iconic artworks, and vibrant interiors that reflect her passion and resilience. This is one of the most popular attractions, so booking tickets in advance is recommended.

DAY 5: Xochimilco Floating Gardens

Xochimilco: Experience the enchanting floating gardens of Xochimilco aboard traditional, colorful trajineras (boats). As you glide through the canals, enjoy live mariachi music, indulge in local food sold from nearby boats, and bask in the festive ambiance. It's a cultural experience not to be missed.

DAY 6: Basilica of Our Lady of Guadalupe

Basilica of Our Lady of Guadalupe: One of the most important pilgrimage sites for Catholics worldwide, this basilica is dedicated to Mexico's patron saint, the Virgin of Guadalupe. Marvel at the striking modern and traditional architecture, as well as the miraculous image of the Virgin Mary.

DAY 7: Polanco and Soumaya Museum

Polanco: Visit one of Mexico City's upscale neighborhoods, where you'll find luxury boutiques, gourmet restaurants, and cultural landmarks. Take a leisurely walk along Avenida Masaryk and enjoy the cosmopolitan atmosphere.

Soumaya Museum: This striking building is a work of art in itself, housing a vast collection of European and Mexican masterpieces. Admission is free, making it an excellent option for budget-conscious travelers.
