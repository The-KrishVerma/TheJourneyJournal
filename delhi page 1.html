Best Things To Do In Delhi, India (With Insider Tips!)

Last updated: January 25, 2025



Delhi, the heart of India, is a city that beautifully blends history, culture, and modernity. As the capital of India, it is home to a treasure trove of architectural marvels, bustling markets, and mouthwatering cuisine. Whether you are a history enthusiast, a foodie, or just someone looking to soak in the vibrant culture, Delhi has something for everyone. With landmarks that date back centuries and a cosmopolitan buzz that ensures every moment feels like a new discovery, the city offers an intriguing mix of old-world charm and contemporary elegance. Take a walk through its alleys, discover local art and cuisine, and experience the hustle and bustle of its crowded streets, each corner of Delhi tells a unique story that reflects its rich and diverse history. Let’s explore some of the top attractions that make Delhi an unforgettable destination!

Top 5 Places To Visit In Delhi In 2025:

1. The Red Fort

The Red Fort, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, symbolizes India’s rich history and independence. Built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in 1648, its red sandstone walls enclose stunning palaces, gardens, and museums. Serving as the seat of the Mughal emperors for 200 years, the fort showcases iconic Mughal architecture with high ceilings, intricate carvings, and sweeping courtyards. Don’t miss the evening light and sound show narrating the fort's history and its role in shaping India’s political landscape.

Tickets are INR 50 for Indian nationals and INR 600 for foreign tourists. Open 9:30 AM to 4:30 PM daily, except Monday.

2. Lotus Temple

The Lotus Temple, a stunning Baháʼí House of Worship, is renowned for its lotus-like design. This architectural marvel offers a peaceful sanctuary where people of all religions can meditate and reflect. The pristine white marble structure is especially captivating in the evening when glowing lights enhance its beauty. Surrounded by lush gardens, the temple provides a serene environment for introspection, amplified by the silence within its premises.

3. Qutub Minar

Qutub Minar is one of Delhi’s most iconic landmarks and the world’s tallest brick minaret. This magnificent structure, built in 1193 by Qutab-ud-din Aibak, is surrounded by several other historical monuments, including the Quwwat-ul-Islam mosque. The 73-meter-tall tower stands as a testament to India’s rich history, and visitors can admire its intricate inscriptions and stunning carvings as they ascend the spiral staircase (currently closed to the public). This UNESCO World Heritage Site highlights the Islamic architectural influence in the Indian subcontinent and represents the enduring legacy of India's diverse cultural heritage.

4. India Gate

India Gate, a grand war memorial, honors Indian soldiers who died during World War I. Located in the city’s heart, it is surrounded by lush gardens and offers a peaceful ambiance. At night, the illuminated structure highlights its architectural details. Often called "the ceremonial center," the area features avenues, historical structures, and a serene setting for evening walks. It remains a revered symbol of resilience, visited by millions who pay homage to India’s martyrs.

5. Chandni Chowk

Chandni Chowk, one of Delhi’s oldest and busiest markets, is renowned for its chaotic charm, vibrant shops, and delicious street food. Dating back to the Mughal era, it offers a glimpse into traditional Indian commerce with merchants selling everything from clothes to electronics. Visitors can savor paranthas, jalebis, and kebabs from local eateries while exploring Delhi’s authentic food scene, local crafts, and souvenirs.

Local Tips:

Indulge in authentic North Indian cuisine at Karim’s near Jama Masjid, a legendary eatery loved by locals and tourists alike. The rich flavors, aromatic spices, and traditional Mughlai preparation styles of Karim’s will transport your taste buds to another world. The restaurant's decades-long heritage makes it one of Delhi's finest dining experiences.

Use the Delhi Metro for quick and affordable travel around the city. It’s clean, efficient, and well-connected to major attractions. The Metro is one of the most convenient ways to explore Delhi without getting caught in traffic, especially during peak hours.

Visit markets like Dilli Haat for souvenirs and handicrafts. Bargaining is common, so don’t hesitate to negotiate for the best price. The vibrant culture of bargaining adds to the fun and excitement of shopping in Delhi. From handicrafts to intricate textiles, every item in Dilli Haat tells a story of India's craftsmanship.
