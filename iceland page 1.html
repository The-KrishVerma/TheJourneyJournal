Fun Things To Do In Reykjavík Iceland (Plus Tips From A Local!)

Last updated: September 17, 2024



Reykjavík, the capital city of Iceland, is often considered one of Europe’s most remote capitals, nestled in the North Atlantic with a unique blend of natural beauty and vibrant urban life. Despite its distance from major population centers, it has a remarkable ability to captivate visitors with its charm. The city offers something for everyone, from its stunning landscapes, including the Northern Lights and geothermal hot springs, to its rich cultural experiences, such as art galleries, museums, and a thriving music scene. Whether you’re an adventurer seeking outdoor escapades or a traveler in search of culinary delights and cozy cafes, Reykjavík is a destination full of surprises, blending modernity with a touch of Nordic tradition.

Best Things To Do In Reykjavik In 2025:

1. Visit Hallgrímskirkja Church

Hallgrimskirkja Church, one of Reykjavik's most prominent buildings, is hard to miss. This unique architectural masterpiece features a 74-meter tower offering excellent 360-degree views of the city. The church is a monument to Icelandic poet Hallgrímur Pétursson. You can visit most days, except during Sunday services or special events/concerts. The elevator to the top costs 900 ISK ($6 USD) for adults or 100 ISK ($0.70 USD) for children.

2. The Sun Voyager Sculpture

One of my favorite free things to do in Reykjavik is walk along the waterfront to the beautiful metal sculpture resembling a Viking war ship, the famous ‘Solfar’ or Sun Voyager. This remarkable piece of art, created by Icelandic sculptor Jon Gunnar Arnason, is an “ode to the sun,” symbolizing light and hope. The best time to view it is around sunset. You can reach it on foot heading East from Harpa Concert Hall.

3. Harpa Concert Hall

One of the coolest looking buildings I’ve ever visited, Harpa Concert Hall is completely made of glass — reflecting the Icelandic landscape, sea, and sky off its wild geometric design. This impressive structure is home to the Iceland Symphony Orchestra and the Icelandic Opera.

At night there are moving multicolored lights, making the building seem alive! Try to see a concert there, but if not, it’s still worth going in and having a look around (which is free). Harpa is open from 8:00a to midnight. Make sure to walk around the backside by the docks too!

4. Visit The Saga Museum

Want to learn more about Iceland’s fascinating Viking history? Stop into the Viking Saga Museum in Reykjavik’s Grandi neighborhood. They’ve recreated Iceland’s most important moments in history using life-like wax figures. You can explore what life was like for the first settlers through a self-guided audio tour. At the end, you can dress up in Viking clothing for photos! It’s a fun way to glimpse Icelandic history.

5. Feed Birds At Tjörnin Pond

This quiet pond in central Reykjavik is a hangout for people who want to relax and feed the local birds. Reykjavíkurtjörn Pond, or Tjörnin for short, is used by locals for taking a stroll along the shore or walking their dogs. A kind of park in the middle of the city.

The pond is surrounded by Scandinavian style homes, giving it fishing village feel in the heart of the city. There are over 40 species of birds who stop by the pond on a regular basis, including Arctic Tern, ducks, geese, and even Whooper Swans. In the winter Tjörnin freezes over and becomes a giant ice-skating rink!

7. Crazy Icelandic Nightlife

For such a small city, Reykjavik has awesome nightlife. The downtown area’s nightclubs and bars are open late (until 5:00am) on weekends. Everything is based in a central location, so it’s easy to bar hop from one place to the next on foot. My favorite bars in Reykjavik are Kaffibarinn, Kaldi, and the fancier B5.

Iceland is also one of the safest countries in the world, so you can relax and party safely without worrying as much as you might when traveling to other countries. Reykjavik is home to a bunch of cool music festivals too, like the annual Iceland Airwaves in November.

Tips from local:

Try the hot dogs at Bæjarins Beztu Pylsur. This iconic hot dog stand has been a favorite among locals and tourists alike for decades. Order it “eina með öllu” (with everything) to enjoy it the traditional Icelandic way—topped with ketchup, sweet mustard, remoulade, raw onions, and crispy fried onions!

While downtown Reykjavík is charming, venture into neighborhoods like Grandi for unique cafés, art galleries, and the Whales of Iceland exhibit.
