A Guide to Transportation Facilities in Reykjavik

Last updated: January 25, 2025

Exploring Reykjavik is a Unique Adventure!

Reykjavik, Iceland’s capital, offers visitors a rich blend of nature and modern urban conveniences. The public transportation system is efficient, eco-friendly, and affordable, allowing you to enjoy the stunning landscapes while getting around the city.

Top Transportation Options in Reykjavik:

1. Reykjavik City Bus System

Reykjavik has a well-developed and environmentally-conscious bus network known as Strætó. With over 70 routes throughout the city and surrounding areas, the buses are an excellent way to navigate around the city.

One of the biggest advantages of using the city buses is the regularity and efficiency of the services. Tickets can be purchased at kiosks or via the Strætó app, and the cost is reasonable, making it ideal for both locals and tourists. There are also discounts for frequent travelers, such as monthly passes.

2. Taxis and Ride-Sharing Services

Taxis are readily available in Reykjavik, with most stations located near popular spots such as Reykjavik’s bus terminals, the airport, and tourist hubs. Taxis are clean, comfortable, and reliable, but the cost can be a bit higher compared to public transport.

For convenience and a more affordable alternative, Reykjavik also supports ride-sharing services like Uber and Lyft. However, make sure you have a local SIM card for easy access to the ride-sharing apps.

3. Bicycles and Electric Scooters

Reykjavik is known for being a bike-friendly city, with many dedicated bike lanes throughout the city. Renting a bike or an electric scooter is a popular and eco-friendly way to get around. Several rental services make these available for visitors, and they offer a great way to see the city’s landmarks at your own pace.

You can often spot locals cycling to work or running errands, making it an authentic experience for both tourists and residents. The city’s smooth, mostly flat terrain and beautiful scenery make biking a fun activity during summer months.

4. Domestic Flights and the Airport Bus Service

Though Reykjavik has excellent local transportation, Iceland's vast natural beauty often requires quick domestic travel for further exploration. Reykjavik’s main domestic airport, Reykjavik Airport (RKV), connects the capital to small regional hubs throughout Iceland, making it easier to travel to remote and picturesque spots like Akureyri or the Westfjords.

After arriving at Reykjavik Airport, you can take an Airport Express bus service that runs every 30 minutes from the airport to the city center. It's a convenient service for tourists with a cost-effective price, as well as a speedy option to reach downtown in around 40 minutes.

5. Rental Cars

For tourists who plan to venture outside the city to explore Iceland’s stunning landscapes, renting a car is a practical and flexible option. Reykjavik offers numerous car rental agencies catering to different needs and budgets.

Whether you want a compact car to explore Reykjavik and nearby attractions like the Golden Circle or a four-wheel-drive to access the rugged terrains of Iceland’s countryside, you’ll find a variety of rental options. But remember, Icelandic driving conditions can be challenging during winter, so be prepared and always check the weather forecast before you set off.

Tips from Locals:

The weather in Reykjavik can be unpredictable, so always check the weather before using public transport, especially during the winter months when snowstorms may cause delays or cancellations.

Keep in mind that Reykjavik has free Wi-Fi in many public spaces, such as cafés, libraries, and transport hubs. This can be very handy for planning your routes or booking taxis/rideshares on the go.

When taking buses, be aware that drivers may not accept cash for tickets. The most common payment methods are via card, mobile apps, or prepaid passes.
