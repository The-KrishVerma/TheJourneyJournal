Delhi Itinerary (8 Days)

DAY 1: Exploring Historical and Cultural Landmarks

Red Fort: Start your journey at this UNESCO World Heritage site, an iconic symbol of India. Built in the 17th century by Emperor Shah Jahan, the fort represents the peak of Mughal architecture. Stroll through its expansive courtyards, admire the intricate carvings, and learn about India's colonial history in its museum. Read more...

Jama Masjid: A short walk from Red Fort leads you to one of India's largest mosques. Jama Masjid was commissioned by Shah Jahan and boasts an exquisite blend of white marble and red sandstone. Climb one of its minarets for a panoramic view of Old Delhi's bustling streets. Read more...

Raj Ghat: Visit the memorial dedicated to Mahatma Gandhi, where a simple black marble platform marks the spot of his cremation. Read more...

DAY 2: Modern Attractions and Markets

India Gate: Begin your second day at this iconic war memorial located in the heart of New Delhi. Surrounded by lush lawns, it's a great spot for a morning walk or picnic. The eternal flame honors Indian soldiers who lost their lives during World War I. Read more...

Qutub Minar: Head to Mehrauli to see this soaring 73-meter tall minaret, another UNESCO World Heritage site. Built in the 12th century, it features intricate carvings and verses from the Quran, reflecting the grandeur of early Islamic architecture. Read more...

Chandni Chowk: Conclude your day by exploring one of India's oldest and busiest markets. Famous for its street food, jewelry, and textiles, Chandni Chowk offers a sensory overload of sights, sounds, and smells. Don't miss the iconic Paranthe Wali Gali for a delicious end to your trip. Read more...

DAY 3: Lotus Temple and Akshardham Temple

Lotus Temple: Shaped like a lotus flower, this Baháʼí House of Worship offers a serene space for meditation and prayer, welcoming people of all religions. Its striking design and peaceful surrounding gardens make it a popular landmark for those seeking spiritual solace by it's is renowned for its stunning architecture and tranquil surroundings. Read more...

Akshardham Temple: This stunning temple complex showcases the grandeur of Indian culture and spirituality. With its intricate carvings, sprawling gardens, boat rides, and evening light and sound show, Akshardham offers a blend of history, art, and devotion.The central deity, Lord Swaminarayan, is enshrined in the temple, and the museum within the complex narrates stories of India's ancient culture and history.Its architectural brilliance and dedication to preserving traditional values make it a must-visit landmark in the capital. Read more...

DAY 4: Lutyens' Delhi and Hauz Khas

Rashtrapati Bhavan and Parliament House: Explore Lutyens' Delhi, known for its colonial-era architecture. Admire the grandeur of Rashtrapati Bhavan (President’s House) and, if open, stroll through the Mughal Gardens. Visit the Parliament House, a symbol of India's democratic power, to appreciate its architectural brilliance. The area is a blend of political history and architectural splendor, making it an essential stop in Delhi. Read more...

Hauz Khas:Visit Hauz Khas Village, where historic ruins blend with trendy cafes, galleries, and boutiques. The Deer Park and Hauz Khas Lake offer peaceful walks and scenic views, making it a perfect spot for relaxation and exploration. It's a unique mix of history and modern culture, drawing both locals and tourists alike. Read more...

DAY 5: National Museum and Lodhi Garden

National Museum: Explore India’s rich heritage through a vast collection of artifacts, including ancient sculptures, miniature paintings, and cultural exhibits spanning from the Indus Valley Civilization to modern art. It provides an insightful journey through India’s history, showcasing diverse traditions, regional art, and archaeological discoveries that reflect the country’s cultural evolution. Read more...

Lodhi Garden: Explore its historic tombs from the Lodhi era, surrounded by lush greenery, and enjoy the peaceful atmosphere away from the city’s hustle and bustle. The garden also houses several beautiful water bodies and is ideal for morning jogs or an afternoon retreat. With its combination of history and nature, it provides the perfect space for relaxation and exploration of Delhi's architectural past. Read more...

DAY 6: Humayun's Tomb and Purana Qila

Humayun's Tomb: A UNESCO World Heritage site, this magnificent Mughal mausoleum is an architectural precursor to the Taj Mahal. Its stunning gardens and intricate designs make it a must-visit. The tomb, built by Emperor Humayun's widow, reflects the grandeur of Mughal architecture, with beautiful domes and symmetrical layouts. The site is an iconic example of Persian-influenced Mughal architecture and offers visitors a peaceful retreat with its serene surroundings. Read more...

Purana Qila: Explore the towering gateways, archaeological museum, and serene boating area nearby. The fort, dating back to the 16th century, was built by Mughal Emperor Sher Shah Suri and showcases classic Mughal fortifications. With its rich history and tranquil atmosphere, it provides a perfect spot for history enthusiasts and nature lovers alike. Read more...

DAY 7: Shopping and Food Tour

Connaught Place: Discover this historic shopping hub with Georgian-style architecture, flagship stores, and lively eateries. Its central park provides a great spot for relaxation, while the area buzzes with energy, offering both shopping and dining experiences day and night. Read more...

Food Walk in Old Delhi: Join a guided food tour through Old Delhi, savoring iconic dishes like butter chicken, kebabs, and jalebis from renowned vendors. The vibrant streets are filled with rich aromas, offering a unique glimpse into Delhi’s diverse food culture. As you walk through narrow lanes, enjoy the hustle and bustle of local markets and hidden gems. This experience immerses you in the authentic flavors and rich history of Old Delhi’s culinary traditions. Read more...

DAY 8: Day Trip to Agra

Take a day trip to Agra to visit the Taj Mahal, a UNESCO World Heritage site and one of the Seven Wonders of the World. Witness its timeless beauty during sunrise, when the monument’s white marble glistens in the early morning light. Explore other nearby attractions like Agra Fort, a historic Mughal fortress with stunning views of the Taj Mahal, and Mehtab Bagh, a serene garden offering a unique view of the Taj across the Yamuna River. Additionally, experience the rich history of the city through its local markets, traditional crafts, and authentic Mughlai cuisine. Agra is also known for its intricate marble inlay work, which makes for great souvenirs. Don’t miss the chance to enjoy a peaceful boat ride along the Yamuna River for a different perspective of the Taj. The day trip also offers the chance to explore the bustling local markets, where you can shop for handmade crafts and souvenirs unique to Agra.
