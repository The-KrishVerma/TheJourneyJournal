Delhi Itinerary (8 Days)

DAY 1: Exploring Historical and Cultural Landmarks

Red Fort: Start your journey at this UNESCO World Heritage site, an iconic symbol of India. Built in the 17th century by Emperor Shah Jahan, the fort represents the peak of Mughal architecture. Stroll through its expansive courtyards, admire the intricate carvings, and learn about India's colonial history in its museum.

Jama Masjid: A short walk from Red Fort leads you to one of India's largest mosques. Jama Masjid was commissioned by Shah Jahan and boasts an exquisite blend of white marble and red sandstone. Climb one of its minarets for a panoramic view of Old Delhi's bustling streets.

Raj Ghat: Visit the memorial dedicated to Mahatma Gandhi, where a simple black marble platform marks the spot of his cremation. The serene atmosphere is a contrast to the chaotic energy of the city.

DAY 2: Modern Attractions and Markets

India Gate: Begin your second day at this iconic war memorial located in the heart of New Delhi. Surrounded by lush lawns, it's a great spot for a morning walk or picnic. The eternal flame honors Indian soldiers who lost their lives during World War I.

Qutub Minar: Head to Mehrauli to see this soaring 73-meter tall minaret, another UNESCO World Heritage site. Built in the 12th century, it features intricate carvings and verses from the Quran, reflecting the grandeur of early Islamic architecture.

Chandni Chowk: Conclude your day by exploring one of India's oldest and busiest markets. Famous for its street food, jewelry, and textiles, Chandni Chowk offers a sensory overload of sights, sounds, and smells. Don't miss the iconic Paranthe Wali Gali for a delicious end to your trip.

DAY 3: Lotus Temple and Akshardham Temple

Lotus Temple: This architectural marvel is shaped like a lotus flower and serves as a Baháʼí House of Worship. It's a serene space open to people of all religions for meditation and prayer. The surrounding gardens enhance its beauty and provide a peaceful retreat.

Akshardham Temple: This stunning temple complex showcases the grandeur of Indian culture and spirituality. With its intricate carvings, sprawling gardens, boat rides, and evening light and sound show, Akshardham offers a blend of history, art, and devotion.

DAY 4: Lutyens' Delhi and Hauz Khas

Rashtrapati Bhavan and Parliament House: Tour the central administrative area of Lutyens' Delhi, showcasing colonial-era architecture. Snap photos of Rashtrapati Bhavan (President’s House) and stroll through the adjacent Mughal Gardens if they’re open to the public.

Hauz Khas: In the evening, visit the trendy Hauz Khas Village. It combines historic ruins with modern cafes, galleries, and boutiques. The Deer Park and the beautiful Hauz Khas Lake offer scenic views and peaceful walks.

DAY 5: National Museum and Lodhi Garden

National Museum: Discover India's rich history and heritage through its vast collection of artifacts, from the Indus Valley Civilization to modern art. Highlights include ancient sculptures, miniature paintings, and cultural exhibitions.

Lodhi Garden: This picturesque park is a favorite among locals for walks and picnics. Explore its historic tombs from the Lodhi era, surrounded by lush greenery, and enjoy the peaceful atmosphere away from the city’s hustle and bustle.

DAY 6: Humayun's Tomb and Purana Qila

Humayun's Tomb: A UNESCO World Heritage site, this magnificent Mughal mausoleum is an architectural precursor to the Taj Mahal. Its stunning gardens and intricate designs make it a must-visit.

Purana Qila: One of Delhi’s oldest forts, Purana Qila offers a glimpse into its ancient history. Explore the towering gateways, archaeological museum, and serene boating area nearby.

DAY 7: Shopping and Food Tour

Connaught Place: Spend your morning exploring this historic shopping district, known for its Georgian-style architecture, flagship stores, and vibrant eateries.

Food Walk in Old Delhi: Embark on a guided food tour through Old Delhi. Indulge in iconic dishes like butter chicken, kebabs, jalebis, and authentic Indian sweets from famous vendors.

DAY 8: Day Trip to Agra

Take a day trip to Agra to visit the Taj Mahal, a UNESCO World Heritage site and one of the Seven Wonders of the World. Witness its timeless beauty during sunrise and explore other nearby attractions like Agra Fort and Mehtab Bagh.
